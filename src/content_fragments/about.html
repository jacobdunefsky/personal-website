About me.

My name is Jacob Dunefsky. Welcome to my homepage! I am an undergraduate at Yale University pursuing a combined B.S./M.S. degree in computer science.

I am currently...

  • ...working on research regarding mechanistic interpretability for large language models. This research involves a method to find feature vectors in Transformer models without using inexpensive probing or autoencoder methods, and can be used to diagnose the causes of gender bias in language models. A preprint is forthcoming.
  • ...working as a ULA (undergraduate learning assistant) for Yale's course "CPSC 488/588: AI Foundational Model". In this course, students learn about state-of-the-art AI foundation models such as Transformers for NLP and diffusion models for image generation, by engaging with cutting-edge research and implementing this research themselves. My responsibilities include writing problem sets, grading problem sets and exams, and holding office hours.
